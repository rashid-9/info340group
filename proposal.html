Project Proposal: Calorie + Nutrient Tracker

Rashid Bara, Ryan Walsh, Aries Balahadia, Yasin Abdullahi

Problem/Data Domain

Many people want to track what they to lose weight, build muscle, or stay healthy, but tracking calories and macros can be confusing without help. Nutrition tracking is also a data challenge due to people needing acurate food information and a way to link it to their body and goals. Apps like MyFintenessPal show that tracking can help people understand their eating habits, but there apps can feel overwhelming, filled with ads, or locked behind subcriptions. The main challenge is turning data inputted into simple and personal targets. While reliable public nutrition data sources exist, using them in a simple web application requires accuracy and intuitive interface.

App Description

Our proposed web app is a calorie and nutrient tracker made for students and beginners who want something simpler and easier to use than large commercial nutrition apps. Users create a profile by entering basic information like age, height, weight, and activity level, then choose a fitness goal such as losing weight, maintaining weight, or gaining muscle.

Based on this information, the app calculates a daily calorie goal and macronutrient targets using common nutrition formulas and shows them in a clear daily “budget” view. This app is meant to help users stay organized and understand their nutrition without needing a lot of prior knowledge.

Key Features

Feature 1 (significant): Goal and target calculator

The app includes a guided setup form that calculates personalized daily calorie and macronutrient targets. Users can also adjust preferences, such as choosing to focus on higher protein intake.

Feature 2 (significant): Food logging with automatic totals

Users can log foods they eat and choose serving sizes, and the app will automatically update daily totals for calories, protein, carbohydrates, and fat. Nutrition data will come from a small curated dataset based on a reliable source like USDA FoodData Central instead of unverified user entries.

Feature 2.5 (half feature): Progress dashboard

The app shows simple weekly summaries, including average calories and macros, commonly logged foods, and consistency streaks. This helps users notice patterns in their eating habits rather than only focusing on daily numbers.

Users and Impacts

The app is designed to be inclusive and avoid judgmental language. Gender input is optional, and users can add custom foods if the dataset does not match what they eat. This is important because many food databases and health apps do not represent culturally diverse diets, which can make these tools less useful for some users.
